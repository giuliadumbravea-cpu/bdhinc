Starting from Mark Twain’s words

“Don’t go around saying the world owes you a living. The world owes you nothing. It was here first”

we challenge students to reflect on the world: as a place that offers us stability, even though we have abused its natural resources; as a place of infinitely beautiful structures, yet one in which we often value only those features from which we can benefit; and as a place that nourishes civilisations while bearing witness to their self-destruction.

We live in a world of so many opportunities that we not only minimalize and take for granted, but also come to believe that we are entitled to them.
